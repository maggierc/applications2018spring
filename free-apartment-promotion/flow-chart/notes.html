Additional Guidelines:

  • this is a microsite
  • define the happy path (the way you hope the user to go)
  • assume that EZ has a cyclical email process, big push into spring/summer and then emails die off
  • EZ Properties has moderate properties, similar to loop style apartments
  • point of promotion is to gather emails: anyone who loses becomes a new contact for EZ
  • understood that entering the sweepstakes means joining the newsletter (may need explicit wording)
  • how will the website behave depending on what i do as a user? (NOT LINEAR)
    • i.e. if they don't put in a valid email, you should tell them
    • when user puts in email, they should move on to a screen that confirms it
      • say thanks!
    • choices offered up: back up, check out fine print, give options to get extra info to ease hesitations
    • what happens if they've already signed up?
      • sorry, you've already entered thAnkS aNYwaY
    • if you made a mistake in the form, it should STAY on the form instead of just moving on
    • have spots that suggest user to check out properties ("start thinking about where you want to live without rent!")
    • starting with about / immediate sign up
  • show map of properties at some point?
  • specific dates the user should know about
    • IDEA: show calendar of how far away user is from sweepstakes? countdown timer?
    • what does site look like before?
    • during?
    • after submission but before announcement?
      • i.e. can't see winner announcement page before anyone wins
    • for announcement?
    • after everythings over?

Getting into the project:

  • how do people interact with devices
  • look up other sweepstakes/promotions
  • look up microsites
  • why would people avoid promotions?
  • look at advertising campaigns (spotify?)
  • is submitting an email a good thing?
  • how do we make EZ Properties look good?
  • eligibility can be confirmed AFTER win, so you only really need to have people submit ways to contact them
  • how much information do users need to engage fully? how little information do they need?

balance competing needs of user and company

  • user wants to give away as little information as possible to win; company wants to gain as much information as possible in order to continue contacting students

extending past visual design, important to understand what users want and how to COMMUNICATE to them
